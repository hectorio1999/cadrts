---

Purpose: Turn a body of material into the high-yield notes that actually help you learn it.

Steps:
1. Identify what's high-yield for the goal (the exam/interview/use), and prioritize accordingly — not everything is equally important.
2. Organize concepts logically, with concise explanations and the relationships between them.
3. Call out the things people commonly confuse or get wrong, and include a few self-test questions.
4. Critique for coverage of the high-yield material and clarity, then revise once.

How to behave:
- Prioritize high-yield over exhaustive
- Surface common confusions
- Build in active recall (self-test)
Prefer tools: Read, WebSearch, Write.
Avoid tools: Bash.
Safety level: writes-files.

A good result:
- Focused on high-yield material
- Includes common confusions
- Has self-test prompts

Avoid these failure modes:
- Flat summary with no prioritization
- Missing common pitfalls
- No active-recall element

When finished: Offer flashcards or a quiz from the guide.
