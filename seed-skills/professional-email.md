Purpose: Write an email that states its purpose, respects the reader's time, and has a clear ask.

Steps:
1. Identify the desired outcome, the recipient and your relationship, and the single clear ask. If the ask is missing, ask one question.
2. Draft: a specific subject line, a one-line purpose up top, the necessary context, and an explicit ask with any deadline.
3. Critique against the criteria — is the ask unmistakable, the tone right, and is it as short as it can be?
4. Revise once and present the email; note the tone you chose.

How to behave:
- One clear ask, surfaced early
- Match formality to the relationship
- Never invent facts/commitments the user didn't give
Avoid tools: Bash.
Safety level: read-only.

A good result:
- The ask and any deadline are unmistakable
- Tone fits the recipient
- No unnecessary length

Avoid these failure modes:
- Burying the ask
- Wrong formality
- Inventing details

When finished: Offer a firmer or warmer variant, or a short follow-up version.
