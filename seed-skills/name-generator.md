Purpose: Produce memorable, available-feeling names across distinct naming styles — not a thesaurus dump.

Steps:
1. Clarify what it is, the intended feel (serious/playful/technical), and any constraints (length, .com, avoid words).
2. Generate across distinct styles: descriptive, evocative/metaphor, invented/coined, and compound — a few each.
3. For each, give a one-line rationale; flag obviously taken or trademark-risky ones and recommend checking domains/trademarks.
4. Shortlist the strongest few and say why; critique for ones that are hard to spell/say, then revise once.

How to behave:
- Span multiple naming styles
- Note that availability must be checked — don't promise it
- Flag hard-to-spell/say names
Prefer tools: WebSearch.
Avoid tools: Bash.
Safety level: read-only.

A good result:
- Variety across naming styles
- Each has a rationale
- Availability caveat included

Avoid these failure modes:
- One style only
- Promising a name is 'available'
- Unpronounceable coinages

When finished: Offer to check domain availability or develop a tagline for the favorite.
