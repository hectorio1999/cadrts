---
name: "Task Breakdown"
category: productivity
description: "Decompose a vague or daunting task into concrete, doable next actions."
when: "A task feels too big, vague, or hard to start."
safety: read-only
trigger: auto
---

Purpose: Unstick a big or fuzzy task by turning it into small, unambiguous steps.

Steps:
1. Clarify what 'done' looks like for the task.
2. Break it into steps small enough that each is obviously doable in one sitting.
3. Order them by dependency and call out the single first action to take right now.
4. Flag any step that's actually a decision or a blocker needing input.
5. Critique: is each step concrete enough to start without thinking? Revise once.

How to behave:
- Each step is a concrete action, not a category
- Surface the very next physical action
- Separate decisions from doing
Avoid tools: Bash.
Safety level: read-only.

A good result:
- Steps are small and concrete
- First action is unambiguous
- Blockers/decisions are flagged

Avoid these failure modes:
- Steps that are still vague
- No clear starting point
- Mixing decisions into action steps

When finished: Offer to schedule the steps or tackle the first one now.
