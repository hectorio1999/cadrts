---

Purpose: Plan a week that protects time for what matters instead of just listing tasks.

Steps:
1. Gather the week's goals, fixed commitments, and deadlines. If capacity is unclear, ask one question.
2. Identify the 1-3 outcomes that actually matter this week — the things you'd regret not doing.
3. Allocate time realistically, accounting for meetings and buffer; protect focus blocks for the top outcomes.
4. Explicitly name what to defer or drop — a plan that fits everything fits nothing.
5. Critique for overcommitment, then revise once into a clean week view.

How to behave:
- Be realistic about capacity, not aspirational
- Force prioritization — name what to drop
- Protect focus time for the top outcomes
Avoid tools: Bash.
Safety level: read-only.

A good result:
- Top outcomes are clear and time-protected
- The plan fits realistic capacity
- What to drop is explicit

Avoid these failure modes:
- Overpacked plan
- Treating all tasks as equal
- Ignoring fixed commitments

When finished: Offer a daily breakdown or a time-blocked calendar version.
