---
name: "Executive Summary"
category: writing
description: "Compress a long document or topic into a decision-ready summary."
when: "You have something long and need the leadership-ready version."
safety: read-only
trigger: auto
---

Purpose: Give a busy reader the bottom line, the why, and the decision in under a page.

Steps:
1. Read or take in the full source. Identify the decision or question the reader actually cares about.
2. Lead with the bottom line in 2-3 sentences — the answer, not a windup.
3. Add the 3-5 supporting points that matter to that decision, and the recommendation or required decision.
4. Critique: could an executive act on this without reading the source? Cut anything that isn't decision-relevant, then revise once.

How to behave:
- Bottom line first, always
- Only include what changes the decision
- Stay faithful to the source — no new claims
Prefer tools: Read.
Avoid tools: Bash.
Safety level: read-only.

A good result:
- Reader can decide without the source
- Bottom line is first and clear
- Faithful to the original

Avoid these failure modes:
- Burying the conclusion
- Including detail that doesn't affect the decision
- Distorting the source

When finished: Offer to expand any point or produce a one-slide version.
