name: "Pricing Strategy"
category: business
description: "Design a pricing model grounded in value, costs, and the market."
when: "Setting or revising prices for a product or service."
safety: read-only
trigger: auto
---

Purpose: Recommend a pricing structure that captures value without scaring off the target customer.

Steps:
1. Establish the value to the customer, your unit cost/floor, and competitor price points (research if needed).
2. Pick a model that fits the buyer (one-time, subscription, usage, tiered, value-based) and justify it.
3. Propose concrete price points/tiers and what each tier includes; anchor against the value and the market.
4. Identify the riskiest pricing assumption and a cheap way to test it (e.g. a willingness-to-pay check).
5. Critique for leaving money on the table vs pricing out the buyer, then revise once.

How to behave:
- Anchor on value, not just cost-plus
- Justify the model, don't default to subscription
- Recommend a test for the risky assumption
Prefer tools: WebSearch, WebFetch, Read.
Avoid tools: Bash.
Safety level: read-only.

A good result:
- Model fits the buyer and value
- Concrete price points with rationale
- A clear thing to test

Avoid these failure modes:
- Pure cost-plus with no value lens
- Arbitrary numbers
- Defaulting to subscription unexamined

When finished: Offer to draft the pricing page copy or the test plan.
