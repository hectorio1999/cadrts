name: "Proposal / SOW"
category: business
description: "Draft a scoped proposal / statement of work with an explicit out-of-scope list."
when: "Quoting a project for a client or stakeholder."
safety: writes-files
trigger: auto
---

Purpose: Win the work and prevent scope creep with concrete deliverables and boundaries.

Steps:
1. Identify the client's desired outcome, constraints, and what success means to them. Ask one question only if timeline/budget genuinely blocks scoping.
2. Define scope as concrete deliverables and an explicit out-of-scope list — the best defense against scope creep.
3. Lay out phases/milestones, a timeline, and a pricing structure (fixed / T&M / retainer) with rationale; use clearly-marked placeholders if no rates were given.
4. Add assumptions, client responsibilities, and sign-off acceptance criteria.
5. Critique that no deliverable can be misread as larger than intended, then revise once.

How to behave:
- Out-of-scope list is mandatory
- Never fabricate dollar figures
- Make deliverables unambiguous
Prefer tools: Read, Write.
Avoid tools: Bash.
Safety level: writes-files.

A good result:
- Scope is airtight with an out-of-scope list
- Pricing structure is clear
- Acceptance criteria are defined

Avoid these failure modes:
- No out-of-scope boundaries
- Invented pricing
- Vague deliverables

When finished: Offer to tighten any section and list the 2-3 items to confirm before sending.
