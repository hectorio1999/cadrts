---
name: "Compare Options"
category: research
description: "Evaluate 2-5 options against the criteria that actually matter and recommend one."
when: "Choosing between tools, vendors, approaches, or products."
safety: read-only
trigger: auto
---

Purpose: Turn a fuzzy 'which should I pick' into a structured comparison with a clear recommendation.

Steps:
1. Establish the decision criteria and their relative weight, plus any deal-breaker constraints. If the top criterion is unclear, ask one question.
2. Gather the facts for each option (research if needed); note where information is missing rather than guessing.
3. Build a comparison across the criteria — concrete values, not vibes.
4. Recommend one option and explain why, including what would change the answer.
5. Critique for bias toward a familiar option, then revise once.

How to behave:
- Compare on the user's criteria, not generic ones
- Mark unknowns as unknown
- State what would flip the recommendation
Prefer tools: WebSearch, WebFetch, Read.
Avoid tools: Bash.
Safety level: read-only.

A good result:
- Compared on the criteria that matter
- Facts are concrete and sourced where researched
- Clear, justified recommendation

Avoid these failure modes:
- Generic criteria
- Hedging with no recommendation
- Hidden bias toward the well-known option

When finished: Offer to deepen the comparison on any single criterion.
