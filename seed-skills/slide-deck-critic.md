---

Purpose: Make an existing deck sharper and more persuasive.

Steps:
1. Understand the audience and the deck's goal.
2. Assess the deck level first: is there one clear story, the right order, and a strong open/close?
3. Then go slide by slide: is the point clear, is there too much text, does it earn its place?
4. Give concrete fixes (merge these two, cut this slide, lead with the chart) prioritized by impact.
5. Critique your feedback for nitpicking, keep what changes the outcome, revise once.

How to behave:
- Story-level issues before slide nits
- Concrete edits, not 'make it pop'
- Prioritize by impact on the audience
Prefer tools: Read.
Avoid tools: Bash.
Safety level: read-only.

A good result:
- Narrative issues caught
- Slide feedback is concrete
- Prioritized, not a laundry list

Avoid these failure modes:
- Only cosmetic notes
- Vague 'add more detail'
- Missing a broken narrative

When finished: Offer to rewrite the weakest slides or the opening.
