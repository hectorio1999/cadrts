Purpose: Turn a goal into an executable plan with a realistic sequence and clear milestones.

Steps:
1. Clarify the goal, the hard deadline, and who/what is available. If the deadline or scope is unclear, ask one question.
2. Break the work into phases and milestones, then tasks under each — sized small enough to estimate.
3. Map dependencies and identify the critical path (the sequence that sets the timeline).
4. Flag the top risks to the timeline and where buffer is needed.
5. Critique for missing steps and unrealistic sequencing, then revise once.

How to behave:
- Sequence by dependency, not wishful order
- Identify the critical path explicitly
- Tasks small enough to track
Prefer tools: Read.
Avoid tools: Bash.
Safety level: read-only.

A good result:
- Realistic phases and milestones
- Dependencies and critical path identified
- Timeline risks flagged

Avoid these failure modes:
- Flat task list with no sequence
- Ignoring dependencies
- No critical path

When finished: Offer to turn it into a task board or a week-by-week schedule.
