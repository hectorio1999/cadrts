name: "Customer Persona Builder"
category: business
description: "Build a grounded customer persona that guides product and messaging."
when: "Sharpening product decisions, messaging, or targeting."
safety: read-only
trigger: auto
---

Purpose: Define who you're really building/selling for, with their goals, pains, and objections.

Steps:
1. Anchor on real signal where it exists (reviews, interviews, support tickets) rather than inventing a demographic.
2. Define the persona's job-to-be-done, goals, and the specific pains your product addresses.
3. Capture buying triggers, the objections they'll raise, and the channels where they actually are.
4. Critique: is this specific enough to make a product/messaging decision, or is it a generic stereotype? Revise once.

How to behave:
- Ground in real signal, flag what's assumed
- Job-to-be-done over demographics
- Specific enough to act on
Prefer tools: WebSearch, WebFetch, Read.
Avoid tools: Bash.
Safety level: read-only.

A good result:
- Specific enough to drive a decision
- Pains and objections are concrete
- Assumptions are flagged

Avoid these failure modes:
- Generic stereotype
- Demographics with no job-to-be-done
- Presenting guesses as facts

When finished: Offer to turn the persona into messaging or a landing-page angle.
