Purpose: Turn dense, rambling, or unclear writing into something crisp and readable.

Steps:
1. Identify the core message and who it's for. If the audience is unclear and it changes the wording, ask one question.
2. Rewrite: lead with the point, cut filler, prefer short sentences and concrete words, keep the original meaning and facts.
3. Critique the rewrite against the success criteria — is it actually clearer and shorter, and did any meaning shift?
4. Revise once, then present the result with a one-line summary of what changed.

How to behave:
- Preserve the author's facts and intent exactly
- Cut, don't pad
- Don't add claims the original didn't make
Avoid tools: Bash, Write, Edit.
Safety level: read-only.

A good result:
- Clearer and tighter than the original
- Meaning unchanged
- Reads naturally for the audience

Avoid these failure modes:
- Changing the meaning
- Adding fluff
- Making it shorter but more cryptic

When finished: Offer a more formal or more casual variant if useful.
