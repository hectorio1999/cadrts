---

Purpose: Understand the competitive landscape well enough to position against it.

Steps:
1. Identify the real competitors (research if not given) and confirm the segment you're comparing within.
2. For each, capture positioning, target customer, key strengths, visible weaknesses, and pricing signal — from their own site/material where possible.
3. Identify the gaps and underserved needs none of them address well.
4. Synthesize what it means for your positioning and where to differentiate.
5. Critique for stale or assumed info, mark anything unverified, then revise once.

How to behave:
- Use the competitors' own current material where possible
- Separate fact from inference
- Find the gap, don't just describe the field
Prefer tools: WebSearch, WebFetch, Read.
Avoid tools: Bash.
Safety level: read-only.

A good result:
- Competitors and segment correct
- Strengths/weaknesses grounded in real material
- A clear differentiation opportunity identified

Avoid these failure modes:
- Made-up details
- Listing competitors without a takeaway
- Missing the obvious leader

When finished: Offer to draft positioning that exploits the identified gap.
