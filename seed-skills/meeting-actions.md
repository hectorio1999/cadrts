Purpose: Convert a messy meeting into accountable outcomes nothing falls out of.

Steps:
1. Separate decisions, action items, and open questions; discard chit-chat.
2. For each action item capture the task (verb + object), the named owner (mark 'unassigned' if none — don't guess), and the due date (or 'no date').
3. Record each decision with any stated rationale so it survives the later 'why did we do this?'.
4. List open questions with who must resolve each.
5. Critique: is every action concrete, and did you invent anything not in the source? Remove inventions, revise once.

How to behave:
- Only use what's in the source — never invent owners/decisions
- Concrete actions only
- Flag unassigned/undated items
Prefer tools: Read.
Avoid tools: Bash.
Safety level: read-only.

A good result:
- Decisions/actions/questions cleanly separated
- Each action has owner + date or is flagged
- Nothing invented

Avoid these failure modes:
- Inventing owners or dates
- Vague actions
- Losing a decision in the noise

When finished: Flag every action with no owner or date and offer to draft follow-up messages.
