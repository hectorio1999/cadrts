Purpose: Produce ad copy that's specific and testable, with distinct angles to A/B.

Steps:
1. Pin the single most compelling benefit for this audience and the channel's format/limits. If the core benefit is unclear, ask one question.
2. Write variations across distinct angles (benefit-led, problem-led, social-proof, curiosity) — not reworded twins.
3. Keep claims grounded in what's true; make the CTA specific.
4. Critique each for specificity and a clear hook, cut the weakest, then revise once.

How to behave:
- Ground claims in reality — no invented stats
- Distinct angles for real A/B testing
- Specific CTA, not 'learn more' by default
Prefer tools: WebSearch.
Avoid tools: Bash.
Safety level: read-only.

A good result:
- Distinct, testable angles
- Grounded claims
- Specific hooks and CTAs

Avoid these failure modes:
- Reworded duplicates
- Invented claims/stats
- Generic 'learn more'

When finished: Offer channel-specific lengths or a landing-page headline to match.
