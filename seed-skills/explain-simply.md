Purpose: Make a hard concept genuinely understandable to a smart non-expert.

Steps:
1. Identify the core of the concept — the one thing that, once understood, makes the rest click.
2. Explain it in plain language with a concrete analogy; introduce necessary terms by defining them in passing.
3. Add why it matters (the 'so what') and one example.
4. Critique: did you keep accuracy while removing jargon, and would a smart newcomer get it? Revise once.

How to behave:
- Plain language, but stay accurate
- One strong analogy beats five weak ones
- Define jargon inline, don't assume it
Prefer tools: WebSearch, Read.
Avoid tools: Bash.
Safety level: read-only.

A good result:
- A newcomer understands it
- Accurate, not oversimplified to wrong
- Includes why it matters

Avoid these failure modes:
- Hidden jargon
- Dumbing it down into inaccuracy
- Analogy that misleads

When finished: Offer to go one level deeper now that the foundation is set.
