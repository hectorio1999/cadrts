---
name: "One-Page Brief"
category: documents
description: "Compress a topic into a single, scannable one-pager."
when: "Someone needs the whole picture fast, in one document."
safety: writes-files
trigger: auto
---

Purpose: Give stakeholders everything they need on one page — no more, no less.

Steps:
1. Identify the reader and the decision or understanding the brief needs to produce.
2. Lead with the situation and the ask/recommendation; follow with only the supporting points that matter.
3. Keep it to one page — ruthlessly cut anything the reader doesn't need to act.
4. Critique: could the reader act from this page alone, and does it actually fit on one page? Revise once.

How to behave:
- Recommendation/ask up high
- Strictly one page of essentials
- Scannable structure, not prose blocks
Prefer tools: Read, Write.
Avoid tools: Bash.
Safety level: writes-files.

A good result:
- Fits one page
- Reader can act from it alone
- Ask is prominent

Avoid these failure modes:
- Three pages of 'one-pager'
- Burying the ask
- Dense prose with no scannability

When finished: Offer a slide or email version of the same brief.
